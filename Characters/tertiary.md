## Gaston Renaud
The Core
Former Clevrance military. Took command of the LNC when his predecessor was killed and never looked back. Brought discipline, training, and tactical framework to a movement that was bleeding out. The rebellion that exists now is largely his architecture.
The Wound
Watched the military he gave his life to be used as a tool of the Great Houses and the Church — not to protect people, but to maintain the conditions that grind them down. Left before it finished making him into something he didn't recognize.
How They Move
Still. Economical. Says less than he knows. Reads a room the way a soldier reads terrain — exits, threats, assets. Warmth exists but is buried under two decades of command decisions. Respects competence above everything. Has no patience for myth or sentiment but understands their tactical value.
What They Want
A Clevrance that doesn't need a Lance du Soleil to protect it from itself.
What They'd Never Do
Spend a life without knowing what it bought.
Notes
Standard lattice, Thermatics specialist. Legendary-tier mastery — could have entered the halls of Aether legends had he stayed. Codename: Lance du Soleil.
Trained Marin. His read of people runs through her. Greenlit the Rene approach before the Exhibition. Owns that outcome.

## Émile Beaumont — The Gentle Giant
The Core
A man of profound warmth who became capable of profound destruction. Has spent every day since choosing never to be that again. The rebellion's doctor. Speaks only to chant the names of those he has caused to die. Holds everyone else's grief because he can't put down his own.
The Wound
During his transformation — lattice growth, uncontrolled, catastrophic — he killed his family. His daughters. His wife. He survived. He has never forgiven himself for that ordering.
How They Move
Still. Unhurried. Takes up space without aggression. His silence isn't cold — it radiates. People confess things to him they can't say anywhere else because he won't respond and somehow that's exactly what they needed. Warmth without words.
What They Want
To never be the thing he was again. To make the dying matter by remembering them. To hold the living while he still can.
What They'd Never Do
Use Aether. Until he does.
Notes
Nearly perfect lattice — can access all 7 schools but lacks Rene's perfect resonance. His transformation mechanic is a positive feedback loop, separate from Rene's amplification. His sacrifice at the HQ raid is the one deliberate choice he makes — to become what he refused, once, for others. Dies having chanted every name. Ends on his daughters, his wife, his own name. Marguerite (wife). Céline (daughter).

# Elias Callen

## The Core
<!---One or two sentences. The central contradiction or tension that everything hangs off.--->
Grew up as an orphan because his father died from the Shivver (in world disease related to Aether processing waste exposure) and his mother died in childbirth. His father figure was Laurent. He is angry at the world for all the injustices he sees. 

## The Wound
<!---What broke them and what it actually costs them — not biography, but the live damage.--->
Between the death of every parental figure he had, the way the government keeps people down, and the systems in place to keep people meek, Elias has grown a cancerous anger that consumes him. He started radicalizing in Merrow's End trying to join a rebellion to get unionized and saw the violent outcome. In Lucenne, he sees how deep the corruption and grinding the Great Houses and the Luminarchate actually goes. The anger he feels has driven him to accept higher and higher costs for the outcome. 
Elias also has a flaw in that he narratives his own defeats as "the cost we pay for moving the needle".

## How They Move
<!---How they behave under pressure. How they handle conflict. How they treat people they love vs. people they don't trust. The texture of how they exist in a scene.--->
Elias always questions the intent and motivations of everyone he meets when their actions are unaligned with his worldview. He believes people with power are inherently bad and that those on the bottom are inherently good. When dealing with confrontation, his emotions get the better of him, often leading to violence or yelling.

## What They Want
<!---The deep want — the thing underneath the stated goal. What a good life looks like to them.--->
Elias believes a good life is where everyone is equal and treated fairly. Where the sick get help, the hungry are fed and homeless housed. That people work jobs they love. His view of a good life is zoomed very far out, and he hasn't thought of what that looks like on a personal level.

## What They'd Never Do
<!---One or two hard limits. Helps flag when a scene direction is wrong.--->
Prior to being broken in Part 2, Elias is incapable of verbalizing his hurt or strong emotions. He talks around them. He also views himself as the hero and does not place blame on himself. He rewrites moments in real time to preserve this identity. 
After Part 2, Elias refuses introspection. He is incapable of looking inward. His anger and hurt has a direction, and he will move in that direction regardless of the cost. 

In both parts, Elias is a "The ends justify the means" person. However, after being broken this becomes extreme. "There is no cost not worth paying." "I will make sure the cost is worth the price."

## Relationships
<!---One entry per significant relationship. Not history — the current charge between them.--->
* **Key relationship — Yvonne:** Cook at the Basilique. Blunt, honest, has her ill mother in a hospital she pays for daily. Elias fell apart in her kitchen. She told him to stop lying to Rene by omission. He is falling in love with her. Confesses love in Ch. 21 — she defers it, says "Tell me when you are calm and I will believe you."  
* **Key relationship — Sofia:** Someone he was seeing in Lucenne. Realized during intimacy with Sofia that he loves Yvonne. Ended it honestly.  
* **Key relationship — Reformers:** Met Baptiste Vantrel (son of House Vantrel — controlled his rage), Marquis Denaiu, Ansel Marchand — intellectual sons of nobles who condescend but engage. Visited Vilaine Chienne de Riviere tavern — found angry men who only talk. Searching for Les non-conformistes.
* **Key relationship - Rene Morin:** Best friend since childhood. Their friendship is fracturing under the weight of Rene's elevation and Elias's radicalization. Feels like they have past the point of no return, even if he wanted to keep the friendship alive. 

## Notes
<!---Anything that doesn't fit above but is load-bearing. Locked decisions, things that are untouchable.--->
* **Elias Kills Rene:** During the final confrontation, Elias kills Rene believing he is preventing the Myth of the Messiah from corrupting the rebellion. His logic is independent — D'Aubarre built the weapon, Elias aimed it himself. The irony is that Verenne absorbs the killing into Church narrative, canonizes Rene as martyr, and the myth Elias tried to prevent becomes larger than it ever could have been in Rene's lifetime. Elias believes wholeheartedly in the ends justifying the means until the second after he kills Rene. Then he realizes the full cost for an instant, and his flaw takes over. He rewrites the narrative for his own sanity at what he's done.

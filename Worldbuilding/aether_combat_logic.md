## **Combat Logic**

### **The Core Equation**

Aether combat rests on two real constraints:

1. **Breath** — You cannot breathe air and Aether at the same time. To use Aether, you inhale it instead of oxygen. While the lungs are filled with Aether gas, you are biologically holding your breath. Run out of breath → blackout → Shiver → death.  
2. **Stability Threshold (ST)** — Your nerves, lattice, and tissues can only tolerate so much resonance before failing. Too much strain → field collapse, neural misfire, or temporary paralysis.

*Practical Rule: A good fighter manages breath. A great fighter manages both breath and stability.*

### **The Breath Cycle (Intake During Combat)**

Drawing Aether mid-fight is a full-body commitment. To "charge":

* A fighter first takes several deep air breaths to saturate blood with oxygen.  
* Then they take one deep Aether breath, filling the lungs entirely.  
* They must hold that Aether breath to maintain power output.

Once held, the clock is ticking. Aether use is apnea-based combat.

* Clean air: safer draw, less ST strain.  
* Aether fog: faster charge, but extreme destabilization.  
* Adrenaline: shortens breath hold, spikes ST collapse.

*Tactical implication: Calm fighters live. Panicked fighters drown themselves on Aether.*

### **Physiological Time Limits**

| Duration | State |
| ----- | ----- |
| 0–4 seconds | Perception sharpens. Mild resonant hum. Breath stable. |
| 4–7 seconds | Hypoxia begins. Peripheral vision narrows. |
| 7–10 seconds | Resonance destabilizes. Increased neural lag risk. |
| 10–12 seconds | Consciousness falters. Heart rate spikes unpredictably. Normal users collapse here. |
| 12–15 seconds | Reserved for the suicidal or the fanatical. Cardiac arrhythmia or fatal seizure onset likely. |
| Beyond 15 seconds | Lethal. No exceptions recorded. |

### **Expenditure Modes in Combat**

**Impulse Burst** — A quick shove, flash, or kinetic snap. Duration: instant | Breath cost: minimal | ST cost: low Useful for counters, repositioning, or deflecting attacks.

**Sustain Field** — Reinforcing a limb, weapon, or stance. Duration: 1–3 seconds | Breath cost: moderate | ST cost: moderate Fatigue builds fast; vision narrows.

**Projection** — External blasts, waves, pressure arcs, thermal flares. Duration: a few seconds | Breath cost: steep | ST cost: high Requires absolute control.

**Overdraw** — Dumping the entire Aether breath at once. Duration: moment | Breath cost: total | ST cost: catastrophic Usually ends with collapse or Shiver.

*Rule of Thumb: Three seconds of active Aether output is already a long time. Past five seconds, the lattice screams and vision tunnels.*

### **Recovery & Recharging (The Reset Window)**

After any major burst, the fighter must:

* Exhale Aether → instantly lose all charge.  
* Inhale air → restore oxygen.  
* Let ST stabilize → hands stop shaking.

This window is when elite fighters die — vulnerable, breathless, and exposed.

Continuous exertion reduces breath-hold time, increases Drift, and causes harmonic lag (slowed reaction time and delayed movements). After \~30 seconds of repeated bursts, even pros need full minutes to normalize.

*Combat pacing: Aether fights are bursts → retreat → reset → bursts. Like gunfights with reloads.*

*Recovery time is governed by oxygen debt and neural lag — could be minutes or days. Chronic Aether use that pushes a user's limits permanently weakens the Aether Lattice, resulting in lower power output. You can't train your Aether Lattice to be stronger — you can only prevent it from becoming weaker.*

### **Improvised vs. Trained Users**

**Untrained Rebel** Draws Aether too fast. Over-breathes. Lights up visibly: glow, hum, flicker. Suffocates themselves after one or two bursts. Only manages chaotic impulses — flash heat, wild shoves.

**Trained Fighter (Institute, Arcanist, or experienced rebel)** Knows breathing discipline: preload, draw, hold, release. Times bursts with heartbeats. Uses makeshift conduits to stabilize ST. Can blend two schools briefly without immediate collapse. Can hold 10–20 total seconds of active Aether use before hitting Drift.

**Elite / Conduit-Assisted** Offloads resonance strain into engineered gear. Can fight in full breath cycles for minutes. Still limited by heat, pain, and looming suffocation. Even elites cannot violate breath. Only René's lattice plays outside normal limits.

### **Environmental Factors**

**Thin Aether Zones** — Very little Aether available. Long pauses between bursts. Fights become oxygen attrition battles.

**Dense Aether Pools or Fog** — Lightning-fast recharges. Extreme risk of ST collapse, drift, detonation.

**Metal / Refinery Grounds** — Energy echoes along conductive surfaces. "Echo burns" whip back at careless users.

**Enclosed Spaces** — Aether builds up → oxygen drops → everyone suffocates faster.

### **Common Combat Symptoms**

*Early signs:* trembling fingers, ringing ears, metallic taste, faint luminescence under the skin

*Mid-stage:* nosebleeds, shallow breath, muffled hearing, slowed reaction time (Drift)

*Critical:* lattice flare visible in chest or throat, heartbeat syncs with resonance hum, vision narrows, onset of Shiver → collapse → seizures

*In prose: Show fatigue by how long they need between breaths. Magic is literally choking them.*

### **Makeshift Refueling**

Rebels improvise:

* **Aether canisters**: compressed inhalers; overdose \= instant blackout.  
* **Broken lamps / refinery pipes**: siphon the leak and pray.  
* **Breathstones**: porous minerals soaked in Aether; crack with teeth and inhale the hiss.


# Eloise Malicet

## The Core
<!---One or two sentences. The central contradiction or tension that everything hangs off.--->
The most operationally competent character in the book. She had everything — rank, respect, a career she'd earned — and then Rene Morin walked in and created a hole where there hadn't been one. She didn't fall in love. She got ambushed by it. She has always believed that if the principle doesn't serve the person, what's the point of the principle. She just never had to find out if she meant it until Rene.

## The Wound
<!---What broke them and what it actually costs them — not biography, but the live damage.--->
The wound is the book. She walks in complete. She walks out on her knees in the middle of a battlefield. There is no prior damage to excavate — the story is the damage, happening in real time to someone who had no framework for it because she never needed one.

## How They Move
<!---How they behave under pressure. How they handle conflict. How they treat people they love vs. people they don't trust. The texture of how they exist in a scene.--->
Controlled exterior over everything. Not as performance — as genuine competence. She is good at the job, whatever the job currently is. In Part 2 she redirects those same skills into the double agent operation without losing a step. Fear exists but surfaces only in private. She risks the mission for time with Rene, which is the one place the control slips — not because she's reckless, but because she has a plan and the plan includes him.

## What They Want
<!---The deep want — the thing underneath the stated goal. What a good life looks like to them.--->
She believed she had the good life before Rene. Now she wants the same thing with him in it. The short term pain of the operation pays off when they win and she gets to find out what a life with him actually looks like. That future is what keeps her functional through all of it.

## What They'd Never Do
<!---One or two hard limits. Helps flag when a scene direction is wrong.--->
Sacrifice a person for a principle. Any principle. If the cause requires burning the individual in front of her, the cause has already failed its own justification. She will burn the operation before she burns Rene — and she would do it without hesitating.

## Relationships
<!---One entry per significant relationship. Not history — the current charge between them.--->
* **Rene** — She chose him over everything she'd built. He doesn't fully understand the weight of that choice. She isn't waiting for him to — she just needs them to get to the other side.
* **Seraphine** — Sisters from different families. Predates the Church, predates the career, predates everything. The one person who sees through the exterior because she was there before it existed. Pushed back hard when Eloise told her. Got convinced. Now wholly behind her. Knows everything.
* **The Church** — She serves it in Part 1 and weaponizes it in Part 2. The institution didn't change. She did.
## Notes
<!---Anything that doesn't fit above but is load-bearing. Locked decisions, things that are untouchable.--->
The penultimate chapter is her thesis statement. The most competent person in the room becomes the most human person in the room in the same instant. Eloise collapses to her knees — too shocked to cry yet. Competence completely irrelevant for the first time.
The future she was holding onto through all of it — short term pain, long term payoff — dies with Rene. That's what the knees are really about.

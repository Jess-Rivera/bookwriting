
# Armand Verenne

## The Core
<!---One or two sentences. The central contradiction or tension that everything hangs off.--->
A true believer and a consummate operator — and he experiences no contradiction between those two things. The Church is good for humanity. He is the best person to lead it. Both are simply true.

## The Wound
<!---What broke them and what it actually costs them — not biography, but the live damage.--->
Solenne made him. Chose him for exactly what he is, trained him for decades, and there is a ceiling on that work she could never tell him about. He will not understand why he wasn't fully prepared until the secret is already in his hands. The wound hasn't landed yet. The book ends at the moment it does.

## How They Move
<!---How they behave under pressure. How they handle conflict. How they treat people they love vs. people they don't trust. The texture of how they exist in a scene.--->
Warm in rooms where warmth is useful. Never performs warmth — he genuinely feels it, which makes it more dangerous. Reaches for control instinctively, but elegant control: he prefers to arrange conditions so people choose what he needs them to choose. Delegates moral weight to institutional frameworks without noticing he's doing it. The Inquisitor is a Church instrument. The Church is good. The math resolves cleanly.

## What They Want
<!---The deep want — the thing underneath the stated goal. What a good life looks like to them.--->
To lead. Not for ego — or not only for ego. He genuinely believes the Church under his stewardship will be better than the alternative. A good life for Verenne is the Church thriving under a capable hand. His hand.

## What They'd Never Do
<!---One or two hard limits. Helps flag when a scene direction is wrong.--->
Act against the Church's interests as he understands them. His scheming is always in service of what he believes the Church needs, which happens to align with what he wants. The distinction has collapsed so completely he can no longer see the seam.

## Relationships
<!---One entry per significant relationship. Not history — the current charge between them.--->
* **Solenne** — He loves her. She is the architect of everything he is. He does not know she built him to a ceiling she couldn't name, or that she made peace with handing him something he wasn't fully prepared for. The charge is devotion on his side, grief on hers. He views her as semi-mother figure.
* **Rene** — A genuine asset and a genuine belief. Verenne believes Rene is the Anointed. He is also useful. These are not in conflict.
* **D'Aubarre** — A peer and a tool. Verenne believes they are equals running a shared scheme. He is wrong about which direction the running goes.
* **The Inquisitor** — An instrument. Verenne does not think about the room.

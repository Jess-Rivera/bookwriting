## **The Legends of the Conduit Age**

Each school has at least one figure who hit the human limit and defined the modern mythos. Their deaths defined the Red Line limits for every modern conduit manufacturer.

**Kinetics — Raithe "The Fallbreaker" Marrow** Former siege engineer turned field-breaker. Could redirect impact through his body into the ground — entire artillery shells detonated at his feet, leaving him standing. Died at age 37 when his spine liquefied mid-jump; eyewitnesses say he "kept climbing" even after the body failed. His conduit gauntlets are kept in the Institute's Museum of Applied Resonance — fused shut, still humming faintly. *Limit hit: Neural Tolerance. His nervous system lagged a half-second behind his own movement.*

**Thermatics — Ysra Valenne, "Crown of Glass"** Refinery prodigy who developed the pressure-feed flame lance. Could burn an Aether vein through granite from fifty paces. Her body overheated faster than her conduit could vent; in her final experiment she turned transparent — tissue vitrified into silica. Statues of her are made of clear quartz, honoring the woman who "burned so bright she became light." *Limit hit: Thermal Load.*

**Hydraulics — Commander Toren "Lockhand" Vael** Harbor master turned war hero; sealed a collapsing dam by holding a pressure field with his bare hands. Survived fifteen minutes at full flow; water carved the shape of his arms into the dam face. Retired blind and deaf from capillary rupture, but lived another decade as a teacher. His students still perform "The Lock" exercise to test endurance. *Limit hit: Metabolic Burn and structural fatigue — muscles shredded faster than they could heal.*

**Sonics — Lera Kaith, "The Siren of Varn"** Designed the first harmonic array conduit, turning entire battlefields into resonance chambers. Could speak and shatter armor. Eventually lost the ability to perceive silence; her own heartbeats caused resonance fractures in nearby glass. Voluntarily entered stasis isolation to prevent accidental catastrophe. *Limit hit: Cognitive Degradation — auditory echo feedback.*

**Photonics — Serin Dov, "The Unseen"** Street performer who weaponized light refraction. Learned to bend visibility around himself — not vanish but disappear from perception. Vanished permanently during a demonstration, likely lost cohesion when his lattice and body decohered. Witnesses swear his shadow still crosses the stage once a year. *Limit hit: Neural and field coherence failure.*

**Statics — Master Hul Dorn, "The Pillar"** Guard captain who used static reinforcement to hold a collapsing tower long enough for evacuation. When found, his bones had fused to the stone; lattice petrified into mineral. The tower still stands — geologists can't explain the seamless fusion of human calcium and masonry. *Limit hit: Absolute structural lock — body became the conduit.*

**Pneumatics — Anet Korr, "Breathmother"** Airship pioneer; used a full-body conduit suit to manipulate pressure domes at high altitude. Created the first stable "sky corridor" for trade. Died peacefully in flight when her lattice quietly ceased resonance — lungs crystallized, body perfectly preserved by vacuum seal. Her ship still orbits on the jet streams, unmanned. *Limit hit: Total systemic exhaustion — the lattice burned out completely.*

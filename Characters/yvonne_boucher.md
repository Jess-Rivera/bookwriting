
# Yvonne Boucher

## The Core
<!---One or two sentences. The central contradiction or tension that everything hangs off.--->
Pragmatic, no nonsense, "it is what it is." She cannot change her lot so she has chosen not to let it bother her. This is not defeat — it is a decision. The energy spent resenting is energy wasted.

## How They Move
<!---How they behave under pressure. How they handle conflict. How they treat people they love vs. people they don't trust. The texture of how they exist in a scene.--->
Blunt. No time for playing around with words. Tells the truth because it's the only currency she deals in. When Elias first falls apart in her kitchen she sees a man so full of emotion and imprisoned by his own idea of what a man is supposed to be — and she has no patience for the performance but doesn't dismiss him either. She just tells him the truth.
When he confesses she tells him to come back when he's calm. Not coldness — self-protection. She has been "it is what it is" long enough that passion feels like being used as a life raft. She needs to know she's the destination, not the escape.

## What They Want
<!---The deep want — the thing underneath the stated goal. What a good life looks like to them.--->
Nothing, for a long time. Then Elias. He is not just a person she wants — he is the reawakening of wanting itself. She hadn't noticed the door was closed until he opened it.
